## Design Context

### Users
This is Justin's personal mobile-first dashboard, used mostly on a phone for fast daily checks: weather, market state, news scanning, travel/aviation information, and F1 context. The job is quick orientation, not deep research inside every panel.

### Brand Personality
Calm, precise, premium. The interface should feel like a personal iOS control center with enough financial-dashboard density to support fast decisions without feeling crowded.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the existing Apple-like light/dark neutral system as the primary direction: clean surfaces, restrained elevation, crisp typography, and a single blue interaction accent. Borrow Linear-style information hierarchy for dense market/F1 states: compact rows, clear metadata, subtle dividers, and predictable touch targets. Avoid decorative gradients, oversized cards on mobile, and text-heavy panels that require unnecessary scrolling.

### Design Principles
- Mobile scanning first: compress repeated cards, preserve the most useful metric/title/source/date signal, and reduce vertical dead space.
- Do not disturb the weather experience unless explicitly requested.
- Keep interactivity obvious: all tappable elements should have stable 44px-ish hit areas, clear active state, and no layout jump.
- Use color semantically: blue for navigation/actions, green/red/amber only for market or racing status.
- Prefer progressive disclosure over long default reading blocks, especially in news and market analysis.
